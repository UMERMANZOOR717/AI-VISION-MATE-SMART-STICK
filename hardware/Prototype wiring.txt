  Why a Voltage Divider is Needed
  
  The HC-SR04 ECHO pin outputs 5V. The Raspberry Pi 5 GPIO
  pins accept a maximum of 3.3V. Connecting 5V directly would
  damage the Pi. A 1K and 2.2K resistor divider reduces 5V
  to approximately 3.4V which is safe for the Pi GPIO input.
  
  CENTER SENSOR  (GPIO17 / GPIO27)
  VCC  -- Red wire    -- Pi Pin 2  (5V)
  GND  -- Black wire  -- Pi Pin 6  (GND)
  TRIG -- Yellow wire -- Pi Pin 11 (GPIO17) direct connection
  ECHO -- Orange wire -- 1K resistor -- junction point
         Junction     -- Pi Pin 13  (GPIO27) green wire
         Junction     -- 2.2K resistor -- Pi Pin 9 (GND)
  Mounted straight ahead at 0 degrees
  
  LEFT SENSOR  (GPIO23 / GPIO24)
  VCC  -- Red wire    -- Pi Pin 2  (5V shared)
  GND  -- Black wire  -- Pi Pin 20 (GND)
  TRIG -- Yellow wire -- Pi Pin 16 (GPIO23) direct connection
  ECHO -- Orange wire -- 1K resistor -- junction point
         Junction     -- Pi Pin 18  (GPIO24) green wire
         Junction     -- 2.2K resistor -- Pi Pin 20 (GND)
  Mounted tilted 15 degrees to the left
  
  RIGHT SENSOR  (GPIO5 / GPIO6)
  VCC  -- Red wire    -- Pi Pin 2  (5V shared)
  GND  -- Black wire  -- Pi Pin 25 (GND)
  TRIG -- Yellow wire -- Pi Pin 29 (GPIO5)  direct connection
  ECHO -- Orange wire -- 1K resistor -- junction point
          Junction     -- Pi Pin 31  (GPIO6) green wire
          Junction     -- 2.2K resistor -- Pi Pin 25 (GND)
  Mounted tilted 15 degrees to the right
  
  PUSH BUTTON
  Leg 1 -- Pi Pin 5  (GPIO3)
  Leg 2 -- Pi Pin 14 (GND)
  No external resistor needed -- Pi has internal pull-up
  
  Three sensors together cover 45 degrees total.
  Each sensor individually covers 15 degrees.
  Camera field of view is 62 degrees horizontal.
  
  All three red VCC wires are twisted together at Pi Pin 2.
  Each sensor has its own separate voltage divider.
  Sensors fire one at a time to avoid echo crosstalk.
